Integrating Range Overlay Management into Range App (mPR)

Improving Ranging Efficiency and Preventing Accidental Discontinuations

Note: As I left the UK before this solution was fully implemented I am unable to provide details on final outcomes, nor can I present final UI due to commercial sensitivity.

Problem:

  • Buyers used a separate, cumbersome tool (CRST) to execute product overlays, leading to inefficiencies.
  • Accidental product discontinuations could occur if range changes were made at the wrong time, requiring up to two weeks to rebuild ranges.
  • Lack of visibility in CRST regarding the impact of overlays on product distribution, despite agreements to stock products in a certain number of stores.
  • CRST provided no metrics, forcing buyers to rely on manual Excel sheets for decision-making.
  • Inefficient processes, such as needing to remove and re-apply overlays to change a 'victim' product.

Action:

  • Conducted remote workshops with buyers to map user journeys, understand pain points, goals, motivations, and information needs.
  • Collaborated with the lead engineer and Product Managers to prioritise problems and opportunities based on value vs. effort.
  • Mapped out findings in a user story map to guide the first release and future improvements.
  • Ran a two-part, remote design workshop with PMs, lead engineer, UI designer, and users to explore solutions.
  • Utilised Crazy 8s and detailed sketching for ideation.
  • Developed and tested prototypes in InVision and Axure with users remotely.
  • Recorded insights in Airtable to inform design decisions and populate the backlog.

Outcome:

  • Identified critical user pain points and functional gaps in existing overlay processes.
  • Established a clear roadmap for integrating overlay functionality into mPR, addressing key buyer challenges.
  • Developed validated design solutions for a more efficient and error-resistant product range overlay management system.
  • Improved understanding of buyer needs, informing and building confidence in subsequent iteration requirements.
