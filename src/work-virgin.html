Virgin Trains

2017-2018

Role

UX/UI Design Partner

Core Focus

  • Increase digital revenue through upsell to First Class travel, flexible tickets and signposting for price-conscious travellers to more ticket options, such as family and group tickets.
  • Speed up booking for regular travellers.
  • Help customers navigate the jargon of rail to purchase the right ticket for them.

Noteworthy Initiatives

1. Driving £1.14M annual revenue increase by optimising Train Times page

Problem: High traffic (44k monthly SEO sessions) on train times page but low conversion, even accounting for people checking times who have already booked.

Action:

  • Evaluated analytics to uncover optimisation potential.
  • Identified train times page as a high-impact conversion opportunity.
  • Redesigned layout to prioritise booking actions.
  • Simplified ticket option presentation.

Outcome: £95k/month revenue increase (£1.14M annual).

2. Increasing sales and promotional messaging impact through a homepage refresh

Problem: We knew the homepage wasn’t working as hard as it could, especially accounting for the sizeable portion of traffic it garnered.

Action:

  • Contributed to stakeholder workshops to align on objectives.
  • Ran mobile-first design exercises with stakeholders to force prioritisation.
  • Actioned quick and free guerrilla usability testing with public participants.

Solution:

  • Simple, action-oriented links worked best as they were clear and up-front, representing the major jobs people are likely to be doing.
  • Flexible promotional modules provided visibility to non-critical but timely information.
  • Maintained legacy booking form as familiarity and technical constraints trumped a redesign and rebuild.

Outcome:

  • Increased sales.
  • Increased secondary content engagement.
  • Improved promotional flexibility and visibility.

3. Incremental revenue shift through continuous improvement

Approach:

  • Reviewing and analysing analytics, heat maps and user session recordings to identify possible quick wins.
  • A/B testing to validate assumptions.

Example: Added spacing for value-focused copy above booking form.

Outcome: Substantive sales increase worth 10's of thousands of pounds a month.
